About Me

I learned about percentages and proportions in elementary school. I liked how I could divide large categories into parts, ratios, and percentages. It felt like magic when I added up all the parts and they added up to the exact whole that I started with.

Then there was statistics in high school. I could compute basic metrics like the mean, median, and mode of data and use those values to comprehend the entire dataset. I couldn't have fallen more deeply in love. I majored in sciences in college and gained a better understanding of the power of data and statistical analyses.

I kept working on my data skills. First, I realized that each dataset contained numerous insights and that it was critical to understand the problem to find the answers we required. I realized that structured thinking is important in data analysis, probing the data systematically until you find the answers you seek.

Second, it did more than just compute abstract numbers from small datasets. It also entailed using big data to solve real-world problems like resource evaluation, environmental issues, product research, and day-to-day business decisions.

My passion is data, and I work as an analytics consultant to help people and businesses harness the power of big data.
